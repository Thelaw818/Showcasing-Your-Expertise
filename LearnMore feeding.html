Feeding for the Races

Proper feeding is critical to the success of racing pigeons. The right diet enhances endurance, speed, and recovery after races. In this article, we’ll discuss how to optimize feeding practices to ensure your pigeons are race-ready.

Nutrition Tips

  • High-Energy Grains: Include corn, wheat, and barley for carbohydrates that fuel long-distance flights.
  • Protein-Rich Seeds: Add peanuts, sunflower seeds, and hemp seeds to support muscle recovery.
  • Minerals & Vitamins: Provide grit, calcium, and multivitamin supplements to ensure overall health.
  • Hydration: Keep water available at all times and include electrolytes during training and races.

Feeding Schedule

A consistent feeding schedule is key to conditioning racing pigeons. Here’s a sample feeding routine:

Time Meal Details
Morning High-Energy Grains Mix of corn, wheat, and barley to fuel morning training.
Afternoon Protein Boost Peanuts or sunflower seeds to aid muscle recovery.
Evening Balanced Meal Combination of grains and seeds to prepare for the next day.
